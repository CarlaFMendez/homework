    The avocado, known as avocado in much of Latin America, is the pear-shaped and olive-green fruit of the avocado (Persea americana Mill), a tree of the Lauraceae family, to which species such as laurel and laurel belong. cinnamon. There are approximately 400 varieties of avocado trees, so their fruits have very different shapes and weights, which can reach a maximum of 2 kg. The main varieties marketed in Argentina are Hass and Pinkerton, both with smaller and rough skins, and Bacon, larger and with smooth skin. The green color of the skin depends on the variety, there are lighter and more intense, but they all darken as they mature.







    The avocado is a fruit rich in fiber (5%) and healthy fats (15%). Thanks to its high oleic acid value, it exerts beneficial cardiovascular effects, reducing plasma cholesterol levels. In addition, it provides a good amount of vitamins and minerals. It has monounsaturated fatty acids that can help maintain cardiovascular health.
